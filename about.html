Gemma Ellis

Teacher

A British Wheel of Yoga Teacher. I have been practicing yoga for over 15 years. My practice has helped me grow stronger and more flexible, both on and off the mat. With a background in sports, fitness and education, I want students to have the confidence to bring balance in their lives. My aim is to create a comfortable environment for students to develop a connection with their bodies. It is a non competitive class with the focus on them as individuals. I also want students to strengthen muscles, improve flexibility and overall have an enhanced feeling of well-being.

Tips

  • What to wear: Please wear close-fitting and comfortable attire.
  • What to bring: Bring a bottle of water and mat. Please bring a blanket for the relaxation. Please complete a New Client Form before your first class.
  • What to expect: Classes will start with breath awareness. Then progress onto traditional yoga asanas, finishing with a relaxation.
  • Food and drink: Please avoid eating a heavy meal 1 hour before class.
  • Keep up to date: Please check my Facebook page ‘Gemma Claire’ for class time tables.

Information

  • Let me know on a weekly basis if your health changes
  • Focus on your breath. Allow your breath to be slow and controlled throughout the practise, the inhalation and exhalation should be the same length.
  • Child's pose is always an option during the session. This is a great resting pose and will help you to relax.
  • Please do not get focused on just being flexible. “Yoga is not about touching your toes, it’s about what you learn on the way down”.

Etiquette

  • Arrive at least 10 minutes before the start of the class.
  • If you do arrive late, please respect the first 10 minutes of breathing exercise.
  • Use the bathroom before class to avoid interruptions later.
  • Avoid heavy perfume.
  • Leave shoes, bags and coats outside the yoga class in the storage location provided.
  • Ensure your mobile phone is switched off or on silent.
  • Respect savasana. Please stay for final relaxation, as this is where much of the benefit will be received.
